# Pilot MoU / Data-Sharing — one-pager template

A plain-language **Memorandum of Understanding** template for a JanVyuha pilot
with a government authority, provided at no cost to the authority. It answers
the "who are you and on what terms?" question that officials raise on first
contact.

> ⚠️ **Not legal advice.** This is a starting point. Have it reviewed by a lawyer
> (and the authority's legal cell) before signing. Bracketed fields are yours to fill.

---

## Memorandum of Understanding — JanVyuha Civic-Reporting Pilot

**Between**
**[Your legal name / registered entity]** ("the Provider"), [address], represented
by [name, designation];

**and**

**[Department / Authority name]** ("the Authority"), [address], represented by
[name, designation] ("the Nodal Officer").

**Effective date:** [date]  ·  **Pilot term:** [8–12] weeks, renewable by mutual
written consent.

### 1. Purpose
To run a **no-cost pilot** of the JanVyuha platform in **[district / area]**,
enabling citizens to report civic and emergency issues that are routed to the
Authority's relevant department(s), and to evaluate routing accuracy, response
times, and citizen adoption.

### 2. Scope
- Departments in scope: [Fire, Police, Ambulance, Municipal, Electricity, Water,
  Animal Welfare — edit as applicable].
- Geography: [district/city].
- Users: general public (open) + Authority-provisioned department accounts.

### 3. Roles & responsibilities
**Provider will:** host and operate the platform on secure infrastructure in an
India region; provision the Authority's admin/department accounts on instruction;
provide onboarding/training and weekly KPI reports; maintain security and privacy
controls; not use citizen data for any purpose beyond the pilot.

**Authority will:** nominate a Nodal Officer; supply department contacts to be
provisioned; act on routed reports through normal channels; provide feedback; and
(once authorised) permit use of its identity for the pilot.

### 4. Data ownership & protection
- **Citizen and civic data generated in the pilot belongs to the citizens and the
  Authority**, not the Provider. The Provider acts solely as a data processor on
  the Authority's behalf.
- Processing complies with the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023**:
  consent, purpose limitation, data minimisation, retention limits, and data-
  principal rights (access, correction, erasure, grievance).
- Data is stored in an **India region**; access is enforced by database-level
  Row-Level Security; evidence media is private.
- On termination, the Provider will **hand over an export** of the Authority's data
  and **securely delete** its copies within [30] days, on request.

### 5. Confidentiality & security
Each party will keep the other's non-public information confidential. The Provider
will maintain reasonable security safeguards, an audit trail, and will report any
data breach to the Authority without undue delay.

### 6. No cost, no exclusivity, no obligation
The pilot is provided **free of charge**. Neither party is obligated to continue
beyond the term. This MoU does **not** create a procurement, a financial
commitment, or an exclusive arrangement, and does not bind either party to a future
contract.

### 7. Intellectual property
The Provider **retains ownership** of the JanVyuha platform and its source code, and
grants the Authority a **non-exclusive, royalty-free licence** to use it for the
duration of the pilot. Authority data and content remain the Authority's.

### 8. Limitation & disclaimer
The platform is provided on a **best-effort** basis for the pilot and **does not
replace** official emergency services or statutory reporting channels (e.g. 112).
To the extent permitted by law, the Provider's liability arising from the pilot is
limited, and the platform is provided "as is" during the evaluation.

### 9. Term & termination
Either party may terminate on [15] days' written notice. Clauses on data ownership,
protection, confidentiality, and IP survive termination.

### 10. Governing law
This MoU is governed by the laws of India; courts at [city/state] have jurisdiction.

**Signed:**

| For the Provider | For the Authority |
|---|---|
| Name: | Name: |
| Designation: | Designation: |
| Date: | Date: |
| Signature: | Signature: |

## If you don't have a registered entity yet

You can still pitch and even run a small pilot as an **individual** — many civic-
tech pilots start this way. But before signing anything binding or handling real
citizen data at scale, consider:

- Registering a simple entity (e.g. a **sole proprietorship / LLP / Section 8
  not-for-profit**) — a Section 8 company reads well for a government civic project.
- Naming yourself as the **Grievance Officer** (DPDP) with a real contact until an
  entity exists.
- Keeping the pilot **explicitly non-commercial and time-boxed** in the MoU (§6).

Have this reviewed by a lawyer — a one-hour consult is worth it before you sign with
a government body.
