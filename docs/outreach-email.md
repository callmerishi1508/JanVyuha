# Outreach Email — final versions + recipient plan

Final, send-ready cover emails. Fill only the **[bracketed]** personal details.
Keep the body as-is — it is deliberately under ~300 words, formal, and makes no
inflated claims. Attach the one-pager and pilot proposal as PDFs; link the live
demo and the video.

**Positioning rules (apply to every message):**
- Never claim government affiliation or authorization. "Independent pilot
  proposal" appears in the signature block of every mail.
- Do not lead with "free" or "₹0". State the operative fact professionally: the
  department needs **no budget allocation, procurement, or hardware** to
  evaluate; the provider hosts and operates the pilot; a **costed scale-up plan**
  is attached. Officials distrust "free"; they respect "no procurement needed to
  evaluate, and here is what scale would cost".
- One recipient per mail, addressed by name and office. No mass BCC.
- Set `VITE_BRAND` to the recipient's state and reseed that state's demo city
  **before** sending, so the first click lands on their geography.

## Sending mechanics

1. **One state at a time.** Rebrand (`VITE_BRAND`), reseed the target city,
   smoke-test on a phone, then send that state's batch. Telangana first
   (Hyderabad data is richest), AP second, TN after verifying the new
   government's IT leadership.
2. **Same letter, three desks per state** (secretary/agency head, minister's
   office, municipal commissioner), each individually addressed. Note in the
   minister-office copy that the department desk has also been written to —
   transparency reads well.
3. **Attachments:** `one-pager.pdf`, `pilot-proposal.pdf` (export from the
   markdown; keep under 5 MB total). Everything else is linked, not attached.
4. **From address:** a professional personal address (or domain mail if you
   have one). No-reply or gmail-with-numbers addresses undermine the letter.
5. **Signature block** must carry your full name, phone, and the demo URL — an
   official who skims will still see the three facts that matter.
6. **Log every send** (date, office, address used) so follow-ups are accurate
   and you never double-send.

### The three answers to have ready before anyone replies
1. **"Who are you / can you sign an MoU?"** — have the `mou-template.md` answer
   decided: individual or registered entity, and who is the DPDP Grievance
   Officer for the pilot.
2. **"What does it cost?"** — "The pilot costs the department nothing and
   requires no procurement; I bear the hosting for the pilot period. Here is
   the costed scale-up analysis." (Hand over `scaling-and-cost.md`.)
3. **"Does it replace 112 / existing portals?"** — "No. It complements them and
   states so in-app; its contribution is correct first-time routing and a
   unified, auditable view across departments."
